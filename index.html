Research Projects

Cheese Engineering

Fungal contamination of soft cheeses, such as feta, poses a significant problem for organic cheese farms. This results in major economic losses, potential health hazards from fungal mycotoxins, and quality reduction due to unnatural odors and flavors. This project focuses on inhibiting the growth of Penicillium commune, a common cause of cheese spoilage, on feta cheese through recombinant chitinase expression in Lactococcus lactis and exploring its industrial applications. My team in iGEM at UC Berkeley is currently working on constructing and transforming shuttle vectors containing our chitinase gene of interest into L. lactis, refining a secretion signaling system, and developing assays that will determine the antifungal activities of our engineered chassis.

Whole-genome Comparative Analysis of Globally Diverse Chlamydia Trachomatis Strains

This research study with the Dean Lab at UCSF analyzed over 800 publicly available Chlamydia trachomatis (Ct) genomes collected from across global sub-continents to identify the Ct lineage-specific biological function and deduce virulence. For the first time, Ct genomes was curated and assembled using a newly-developed nextflow based pipeline called Bactopia. The assembled genomes was used to create whole genome phylogenies and identify patterns of recombination and single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) across the Ct lineages. Fine-detailed comparative genomics will be performed to identify genomic strain types and genes involved in genetic variation, driving selection, virulence and tissue tropism and their impact on chlamydial pathogenesis.

Research Project Proposal: Will Cyclic Activation of OSKM Factors Promote Transient Immunogenicity?

iPSCs hold promising potential for autologous stem cell-based therapies, however, there are drawbacks of iPSCs to be used for therapeutic purposes, including its causation for teratoma formation and immune rejection. By partially reprogramming cells through cyclic activation of OSKM by a tet-inducible system, transient immunogenicity, or the ability of cells and/or tissues to provoke an immune response over a short period of time, can be studied, and its impact can pave the way for the viability of partially reprogrammed cells in clinical applications.
